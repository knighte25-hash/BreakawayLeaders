Late Night Details

Breakaway 2026 | July 17–20
Each Late Night we will need your help to make sure our event is fun, safe, and doesn’t violate the Noise Ordinance of the county (No amplified sound after 10pm). During the Worship and Small Group sessions, our late night team will set up in the area we are hosting the late night. Below you will find details on what each late night will entail. Students should not be anywhere but where the Late Nights are hosted. Please make sure to help guide students back to the correct area if you find them somewhere else.

Kick off the weekend with a Luau pool party night – pack your swim trunks and get ready to kick camp off with a SPLASH! Get your pool gear ready and join us for a night filled with pool games, fun, and more!

We will have three channels of clean music for the kids to pick between. Outside we will have Publix chicken fingers and chips for the kids. As well as glow in the dark Spike Ball. Our Late Night Team may ask for help in serving food. Please keep an eye on students leaving, we want everyone to stay over in this area for the evening.

After Small Groups please have your students head to the Israel Chapel area for an Ice Cream Truck and our Rec Champion final reveal inside of Israel Chapel. Once this night is over, please make sure students are back in their rooms by 11pm AND begin packing and cleaning.

General Dress Code

Our dress code for camp is simple: clean, neat, and modest. Inappropriate clothing is an unnecessary distraction and will be addressed by camp leadership. Here are a few other guidelines:

  • Swimsuits
    • Guys: no speedos or very short shorts
    • Girls: modest, one-piece swimsuits with no midriffs showing. Dress for athletic activity rather than leisure; no clothing that reveals undergarments.
  • No crop-tops/athletic sets that show midriffs, short shorts, or short skirts.
  • No clothing with questionable sayings, slogans, etc.

A good, general rule is when in doubt, leave it out. If you think something may be inappropriate, it probably is. Our leaders will have permission to ask your students to change clothing if guidelines are not met.

Cell Phones

Breakaway 2026 | July 17–20

Dear Parents,

We are so excited you are trusting us with your student this year for Breakaway Summer Camp. Our prayer for Breakaway each year is that this time away will help grow your student in loving the Lord with all their heart, soul, and might. Deuteronomy 6:5-7 says, “You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might. 6 And these words that I command you today shall be on your heart. 7 You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise.”

Although we worship a God who is constant through the ages, we live in a world that is ever changing. Technology is evolving rapidly, and we as your student ministers believe that we must be proactive regarding the use of cell phones in our ministry. God’s command in Deuteronomy is still clear and relevant today: we must teach our students to love God with everything they have. Regarding technology, the mission of DSM has not changed, but the method in which we accomplish this needs an update.

This year, at Breakaway, we will be limiting the use of technology for the entire week. Students are allowed to bring their cell phones to use during the bus ride, but before we arrive at camp, we will collect everyone’s devices for the remainder of the week. On each bus we will collect, label and store each electronic device the students bring with them. These labeled devices will then be stored in the Minister’s rooms inside a locked trunk for the week. Students will receive their devices back when they are on the bus ride back home. Parents will be given the phone numbers of all staff and their student’s leaders.

Our primary motivator for this decision is to allow your student time to grow in their relationship with God. We all know technology is a constant distraction, and we believe without this distraction students will have the opportunity to build deeper relationships with peers and ultimately with Jesus. We also know that Jesus himself needed time to retreat from the daily rhythms of the world, and we desire to create this environment for these exhausted teenagers. Last, we ultimately want to protect their minds and hearts. By limiting access to technology for the week, we can remove any potential inappropriate content or communication from happening at camp.

We understand some students might have some difficulties buying into this vision, but we ask for your support by communicating the benefits of this update to our cell phone policy for Breakaway.

Rules and Expectations

Breakaway 2026 | July 17–20

We are excited and expectant for a great week at Breakaway! To ensure we have the best week possible, the following rules apply for all students.

Immediately Sent Home

  • Use or possession of any illegal substance, alcohol, guns, knives, fireworks, tobacco, e-cigarettes, or related paraphernalia.
  • Student is found inside a room of the opposite sex.
  • Blatantly repeated acts of disrespect for adult supervision or violation of agreed upon specific event rules.

General Safety

  • Students cannot be at any location that does not have an adult present.
  • Students cannot cross the street to the beach without an adult present.
  • Students cannot swim out to any sand bar in the ocean and must stay within 30 feet of the shore.
  • Students must remain within the section of beach reserved for us and cannot explore further. This includes no walks on the beach outside of our designated section.
  • Students cannot leave the property of Cottages Christian Retreat for any reason. This includes no gas station runs, even with an adult leader.
  • Students must travel in groups of 3. A student can never be alone with another student of the opposite sex.
  • No purple.
  • No pranks of any kind will be tolerated.
  • After curfew, students are not allowed to leave their rooms for any reason.
  • In the rooms, represent Christ well. No fight nights, hazing, damaging property, no use of stoves or ovens, no misuse of microwaves.
  • All electronic devices must be turned into your adult leaders on the bus before we arrive at camp.

Students

Come with a heart prepared to learn and to grow closer to God. Be a friend and reach out to others. Be a servant as you look for ways to engage and include others. Enjoy being involved with the different activities and choose to have a positive attitude.

Respect your leaders as well as the community where you spend this week. This includes leaving the bus clean, as well as the rest stops along the way to and from camp. Be attentive and participate during small group sessions and worship experiences. Come expecting to hear the Lord speak into your life this week.

Parents

Pray for your student. In the days leading up to Breakaway and for the time they are at camp, ask God to move in your student’s life and ask that their heart be open to the things God plans to teach them. Talk to your student about coming to Breakaway with a Christ-like attitude.

If a distraction occurs, disciplinary action will be taken. It is never our desire to send a student home; however, we want the focus of the week to be about being and becoming more like Christ.

Breakaway Leadership Committee

Breakaway 2026 | July 17–20

This year, to accommodate our growing camp, we have started a Breakaway Leadership Committee. Please see the following job descriptions to see who you may need to reach out to with specific questions.

Camp Operations Captain: Becky Holt – Oversee all communication and relay information between volunteers and staff. Please see Becky with any questions you may have regarding supplies, student needs, confusion over roles, etc.
  • Logistics Director: Wendy Adams – overseeing all logistical needs for students arriving late and early, safety team and other logistical needs.
    • Nurse: Ashbelle Henry – will handle all medication/ point person for any injury or medical need.
    • Safety and Water Team Lead: Chris Hartin – overseeing team for safety spots, overseeing water and tent teams.
      • Water/Tent Team: Daniel Richardson, Harrison O’Dell, Philip Kaiser, Cole Hartin, Talan Turner, Andy Cartee
  • Small Groups Director: Maile Gowing – main communicator to all Small Group Leaders
    • Female Lead: Danielle Eldridge – see for any female specific dorm needs
    • Male Lead: John Harpole – see for any male specific dorm needs
  • Late Night Director: Jennifer Langston – coordinating all late night activities
    • Food Coordinator: Lauren Beckner
  • Rec Director: Josh Donaldson
    • Rec Team: Graham Gwaltney, Tate Sparks, and Sam Sutton
  • Meal Director: Elizabeth Gwaltney
    • Meal Time Team: Jim Smith and Selected Students per meal
  • Bus Captain Lead and Packing Lead: Leann Turner
  • Supplies Lead: Hannah Armstrong

Meal Time Service:

We are responsible at each meal for serving on the food line. Cottages will provide two staff cooks and our team will need 5+ food servers at each meal. Elizabeth Gwaltney will remind you as a Small Group leader to send 5+ students to serve 10 minutes before the first round of meals. The small group leader selects 5 other students to help stay after and make sure the cafeteria is cleaned properly after all meals are finished. All students are welcome to participate in serving and cleaning – please coordinate with Elizabeth and Jim Smith upon arrival to the cafeteria.
